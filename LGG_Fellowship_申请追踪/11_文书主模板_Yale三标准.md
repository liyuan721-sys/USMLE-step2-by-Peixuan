# LGG 文书主模板 — 按 Yale 三标准搭

> 返回 [[00_总览_行动清单]] · 通用材料 [[02_通用材料准备]] · 技能自学 [[10_分子技能自学计划_2026-07]] · Yale 原文 [[邮件存档/Yale_耶鲁]]

> [!important] 核心策略（2026-07-08 定）
> Yale 项目主任 Bale 点名的**三条录取标准**——**① 临床遗传训练（clinical genetics）· ② 生物信息学（bioinformatics）· ③ 临床遗传实验室 variant analyst 经验**——**不是 Yale 独有，而是整个 LGG 项目的普遍高标准**。
> → **文书主模板一律按这三条最高标准写**，把最强版当基准（master）；其他校只做**减法 / 换称呼**（PS ↔ SOI ↔ LOI ↔ cover letter），不为单校另起炉灶。
> → 好处：一份打到天花板的文书，投哪家都够；Yale 这种"录取极难、100 询问收 1–2 人"的项目也用同一份主稿定制。

## 一、三标准 × 你的材料（对账表）

| Bale 三标准 | 你的现状 | 文书里怎么打 |
|---|---|---|
| **① 临床遗传训练** clinical genetics | ✅ **最强角度**：病理科副主任医师、27,000+ 外科病理签发、4,000 术中会诊、clinicopathologic 整合 | 用"病理临床训练 + 整合形态/临床/分子"顶 clinical 一栏；坦承经典 human genetics 偏弱、正系统补 |

## 二、主模板段落结构（六段，按三标准配重）

```
① 开场 + 资格     ── 意向 + ABMGG 已过 + 绿卡免 sponsor
② 为什么 LGG       ── 分子诊断正重塑病理（IHC 类比）→ 决心投身
③ 根基（标准①）   ── 病理临床训练 + 分子科研底子（gene rearrangement / 39 篇）
④ 刻意搭桥（②③）  ── ⭐核心：正系统建 variant interpretation + bioinformatics + 补 human genetics
⑤ 差异化优势       ── clinicopathologic 整合 = LGG 正需要的相关能力
⑥ 目标 + fit       ── board-certified lab geneticist → lab director
```

第 ④ 段是本次相对 v4 的**主要升级点**（把散在的自学句，重整成"正面命中②③"的一段）。

## 三、主文书草稿 v5（英文，PS / letter of intent 通用基准）

> [!note] 与 v4 差异
> ①②③⑤⑥ 基本沿用 v4 强句；**重写第 ④ 段**：显式分三束打 variant interpretation / bioinformatics / clinical-genetics，其中 **bioinformatics 从无到有**。仍全程进行时（诚实）。落地成 docx 前请通读、把不像你口吻的句子改掉。

**Personal Statement / Letter of Intent (master)**

Yuan Li, MD

I am writing to express my strong interest in the Laboratory Genetics and Genomics (LGG) Fellowship at [Institution] — the field in which I intend to build the remainder of my career. I am a Chinese board-certified pathologist and hold a Doctor of Clinical Medicine degree from Peking Union Medical College, with subspecialty training in pathology and doctoral research in molecular genetics. My credentials have completed review by the American Board of Medical Genetics and Genomics (ABMGG), and as a U.S. permanent resident I require no visa sponsorship.

As a pathologist, I have witnessed firsthand how molecular diagnostics is transforming our discipline. Just as immunohistochemistry once redefined diagnostic pathology, molecular testing — propelled by the rise of precision medicine — is reshaping how diseases are classified and bringing our diagnoses closer to the true biological essence of disease. Increasingly, it is molecular findings that direct the selection of therapy and predict prognosis. I am determined to keep pace with this transformation, to contribute to it in my own right, and to help advance the practice of medicine.

My foundation for this path is a decade of clinical and molecular work. My doctoral research focused on gene-rearrangement studies in Hashimoto's thyroiditis and primary thyroid lymphoma, giving me an early and lasting appreciation for the molecular basis of disease. As a practicing pathologist I signed out more than 27,000 surgical pathology cases and 4,000 intraoperative consultations, and I subsequently served as a Research Pathologist and biomedical scientist at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. I have contributed to 39 peer-reviewed publications and presented at national and international meetings on topics ranging from gastrointestinal pathology to the application of artificial intelligence in endoscopic submucosal dissection — experience that has sharpened my ability to reason from molecular data and to communicate complex findings clearly.

Building on that foundation, I am deliberately developing the three competencies that define the modern genetics laboratory. First, in variant interpretation, I am working through the ACMG/AMP sequence-variant guidelines and the AMP/ASCO/CAP somatic framework, and practicing hands-on curation with the ClinGen Variant Curation Interface and public resources such as ClinVar and gnomAD, so that I enter the fellowship able to classify variants from evidence codes to a final call. Second, in bioinformatics, I am building fluency in the next-generation-sequencing analytic workflow — from FASTQ to BAM to VCF, alignment, variant calling, and annotation, with IGV for visual review — through the Johns Hopkins Genomic Data Science coursework and hands-on practice in Galaxy, so that I can work confidently with genomic data and its pipelines. Third, I am strengthening my knowledge of constitutional and clinical genetics to complement my oncologic and molecular background, from cytogenetic nomenclature and copy-number interpretation to modes of inheritance and genetic counseling principles. This is a structured, ongoing effort that builds directly on my molecular research and prepares me to contribute from the outset rather than starting from zero.

Above all, my training in surgical pathology has taught me to integrate clinical, morphologic, and molecular findings into a single coherent interpretation — precisely the correlation a laboratory geneticist must provide. I am confident this clinicopathologic perspective, paired with the analytic and interpretive skills I am actively building, would let me contribute a distinctive strength to your program.

Laboratory genetics and genomics has been my intended path in the United States from the outset; in 2023 I interviewed for the LGG fellowship at UCLA, an experience that only deepened my commitment, and I am now able to pursue this goal fully and to apply nationwide. My long-term goal is to practice as a board-certified laboratory geneticist and, ultimately, to direct a diagnostic laboratory. I am confident that my clinical foundation, research experience, and demonstrated commitment to this field make me a strong fit for [Institution], and I would welcome the opportunity to discuss my qualifications further. Thank you for considering my application.

## 四、按校"减法 / 换称呼"指南

> 主稿是最长最全版。各校按其**用词**改标题 + 按其**侧重**留/删段，别加东西。

- **Yale（letter of intent，单独精修）**：第 ④ 段的 **bioinformatics + variant analyst 两束往前提、写足**（Bale 明确这两条是你短板，正是他要看的）；加一句命中 Yale 的具体点（DNA Diagnostic Lab / 肿瘤+细胞遗传并重）。**这封别直接套，逐句拔**。
- **PS 家（10 校：Pittsburgh/UW/Columbia/Indiana/Utah/Rutgers/Duke/Stanford/Penn-CHOP/Chicago）**：标题 `Personal Statement`；一页版把第 ③ 或 ⑥ 压缩。Utah/Chicago 明确**一页** → 删到最紧。
- **SOI 家（Greenwood/Nationwide）**：标题 `Statement of Intent`；第 ① 段末加一句"why this program"。
- **LOI / cover letter（Henry Ford=Letter of Interest 或 PS 二选一 / Emory=cover letter / Wisconsin=意向信）**：标题按各校；开头收尾更"信"一点（Dear Program Director / 具体项目名）。
- **UNC**：无独立文书（只 Applicant Info Sheet），但 Info Sheet 里若有陈述栏，用第 ②+④ 段浓缩。
- **绿卡一句**（Colorado/Pittsburgh/Wisconsin/Rutgers/Nationwide/Baylor/Children's Mercy）：cover letter/PS 里点明 "U.S. permanent resident, no visa sponsorship required"（见 [[02_通用材料准备]] §三）。
