# OET 听力 Part A 实战 02 — 皮肤问诊 (Q1–12) + 关节问诊 (Q13–24)

> 返回 [[00_OET总览]] · 生词拼写 → [[OET_听力_默写词库]] · 通用技巧 → [[OET_听力阅读_技巧]]

> [!info] 素材与做法
> 两段 GP (general practitioner, 全科医生) / specialist 问诊（同一套 Part A 练习，来源：OET Global Fighters 样题）。做法：先盲做填空 → 对照本笔记 §答案分析 → 精听逐字稿跟读，不认识的词进 [[OET_听力_默写词库]]。转写中 D = Doctor（医生），P = Patient（患者）。
> - Extract 1：皮肤增厚红斑数月（John Stark，tour guide 导游）— 指向 psoriasis（银屑病）
> - Extract 2：反复关节损伤 2 年（Chris Davis，student 学生）— 关节 + 肢端发冷；含痤疮 acne 用药史（isotretinoin 异维A酸线索）
>
> [!warning] 录音方法教训（2026-07-05）
> 本套音频**在电脑上外放**。初次用麦克风隔空录 → 音量极低（RMS 0.003）、后半段全丢，逼我靠临床常识猜 Q6/7/9/11 → **错 4 个**。改用 **WASAPI 内录（loopback，抓电脑输出）** 后音频满幅清晰、12+12 空全部亲耳确认。以后电脑里放的音频一律内录。详见记忆 [[reference_audio_record_transcribe_workflow]]。

## 一、Extract 1 — John Stark（皮肤增厚红斑）

### 1.1 完整 Transcript

（题头与患者信息播报在录音开始前，未收录；转写从患者主诉开始。）

**P**: Well, I've got these skin problems which seem to be getting worse. At first I thought I had <font color="#ff0000">dermatitis</font> or maybe even a <font color="#ff0000">fungal infection</font>. I'd had something like that as a child. Then I started noticing these thick <font color="#ff0000">red areas</font> on my skin. They're sort of a little <font color="#ff0000">raised</font>, I guess. They just got worse and the affected area got bigger and bigger all the time. I was a bit worried that it might be <font color="#ff0000">contagious</font>, you know. I was worried I'd picked up something from one of my young <font color="#ff0000">nieces</font>. I see them quite a lot.
**D**: And these patches are?
**P**: They're mainly on the outside of my <font color="#ff0000">elbow</font> and over my <font color="#ff0000">kneecaps</font>. Oh, and on my <font color="#ff0000">neck</font>.
**D**: Right.
**P**: I've been wearing long-sleeved shirts and jeans to cover them up, but I can't hide those so everyone can see them. They're also very painful. Sometimes it feels as if my skin's <font color="#ff0000">burning</font>. It's very <font color="#ff0000">itchy</font> as well. I try not to <font color="#ff0000">scratch</font> it, but it's almost impossible, especially at night. Sometimes it's so bad that I find it hard to get comfortable and I <font color="#ff0000">can't sleep</font>. It leaves me tired most of the time, and that has an impact on my work during the day, quite apart from the <font color="#ff0000">embarrassment</font> of the symptoms themselves. I'm a <font color="#ff0000">tour guide</font>, you see, so I'm very much <font color="#ff0000">in the public eye</font>.
**D**: Yes, I imagine that it's been difficult for you. Have you tried taking anything?
**P**: Well, I tried to fix the problem myself at first. I bought some cream from the <font color="#ff0000">pharmacy</font> to see if that would help. Sorry, I can't remember what it was called.
**D**: That's okay.
**P**: It didn't do any good anyway, so I tried a <font color="#ff0000">non-allergenic soap</font>, but that didn't make any difference either. I just want to get something which will <font color="#ff0000">clear this up</font>.
**D**: Yes, I understand. Are there any other health issues I should know about? Maybe when you were a child?
**P**: Not really. I used to get <font color="#ff0000">acne</font> as a teenager, but no worse than anybody else, and it cleared up by itself, really. ... But there was something else that I didn't tell my GP about. ... I went away on holiday with a friend the summer before last, and we managed to pick up <font color="#ff0000">scabies</font> from one of the cheap <font color="#ff0000">hostels</font> we stayed in. I was covered in a <font color="#ff0000">rash</font>. It was extremely irritating at night. Now I think about it, it was all over my body, especially around my <font color="#ff0000">midriff</font>. It looked like I was covered in little <font color="#ff0000">pimples</font>. Fortunately, we got rid of it using a <font color="#ff0000">lotion</font> from the pharmacy, but I never mentioned it to my doctor.
**D**: I see. And any other health problems?
**P**: Oh, I did have a <font color="#ff0000">bladder infection</font> a while ago, last year I think, but I haven't had any problems since then. Do you think that could have caused my current problem?
**D**: I shouldn't think so. ... I noticed that your GP sent away a <font color="#ff0000">urine sample</font> for testing before referring you to me. The results are quite normal, so I wouldn't be concerned at all.
**P**: Well, that's something, I suppose.
**D**: Okay. What about the effect your current problem is having on your general <font color="#ff0000">mood</font>?
**P**: I'm just <font color="#ff0000">sick of</font> dealing with this. It's really <font color="#ff0000">knocked my self-confidence</font> a lot, and affected my <font color="#ff0000">body image</font> so that I don't go out much anymore. ... it's stopped me from doing things like playing sports or swimming. I feel <font color="#ff0000">lonely</font> most of the time, and a bit <font color="#ff0000">depressed</font> if I'm honest. I'm also concerned that I haven't started any treatment at all.
**D**: I can appreciate your concerns. Now, what I'd like to do is give you a <font color="#ff0000">skin biopsy</font> as soon as possible to see exactly what's going on. I think we can book you in for that early next week.

### 1.2 Q1–12 答案与分析

| # | 答案 | 原文依据 | 考法 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | dermatitis（皮炎） | At first I thought I had dermatitis | 直接抓词；拼写 -titis |
| 2 | fungal（真菌） | or maybe even a fungal infection | 直接抓词 |
| 3 | red | thick red areas on my skin | 直接抓词 |
| 4 | elbow（肘） | on the outside of my elbow | 单数；"outside of" = 伸侧 |
| 5 | kneecaps（膝盖骨） | and over my kneecaps | **精确词**：kneecaps 非 knees |
| 6 | neck（颈） | Oh, and on my neck | **别用疾病常识猜**：银屑病典型是 scalp 头皮，但原文明说 neck |
| 7 | burning（灼烧） | it feels as if my skin's burning | 直接抓词 |
| 8 | pharmacy（药店） | I bought some cream from the pharmacy | 直接抓词 |
| 9 | non-allergenic（非致敏） | I tried a non-allergenic soap | 合成词拼写 |
| 10 | acne（痤疮） | I used to get acne as a teenager | 直接抓词 |
| 11 | bladder（膀胱） | I did have a bladder infection… last year | 见下 danger（压缩+尿检线索）|
| 12 | biopsy（活检） | give you a skin biopsy… book you in… next week | 直接抓词 |

> [!danger] Q6 / Q11 两大陷阱（正是麦克风录废时我猜错的）
> - **Q6 neck**：临床上 psoriasis 银屑病经典累及 **scalp 头皮**，凭"疾病典型"极易填 scalp——但原文明说 "on my **neck**"。**教训：Part A 填空只认原音词，不许拿诊断常识反推答案。**
> - **Q11 bladder**：笔记把两件事压缩成 "picked up scabies from hostels and (11)___ infection"，但原文 scabies（疥疮，summer before last）与 bladder infection（膀胱感染，last year）是**两件独立的事**。两个定位钩子：① 原文 "I did have a **bladder** infection"；② 笔记内部线索 "No abnormalities detected in recent **urine** test"——urine（尿）↔ bladder（膀胱），**光读笔记就能推**。

> [!tip] 临床背景（帮预判 + USMLE 视角）
> thick + red + **raised** plaques（增厚红色隆起斑块）+ 伸侧（elbow 肘 / kneecaps 膝）+ 疼痛灼烧 + 夜间瘙痒 + 影响情绪/社交 → **psoriasis（银屑病）**；确诊金标准 **skin biopsy（皮肤活检）**。患者自己抛出的 dermatitis 皮炎 / fungal 真菌 / scabies 疥疮 / bladder infection 膀胱感染全是**干扰性红鲱鱼**（患者的错误自我归因）。USMLE 视角见 [[完整笔记/Peixuan分科笔记/derm]]。

## 二、Extract 2 — Chris Davis（反复关节损伤）

### 2.1 完整 Transcript

**D**: So, I have your notes from the <font color="#ff0000">sports medicine clinic</font> and it looks as if you've had quite a few problems in recent years. Would you like to talk me through them?
**P**: Okay. Well, the first time I got hurt was two years ago. I took a fall while I was <font color="#ff0000">rollerblading</font> and I hurt my right <font color="#ff0000">wrist</font> here on the side, so I didn't do anything right away, but next day I went and saw the doctor and they gave me an <font color="#ff0000">x-ray</font>, but they said they couldn't see anything wrong and they just gave me some exercises to do.
**D**: Right.
**P**: Then a year ago, I hurt my right <font color="#ff0000">shoulder</font> here when I <font color="#ff0000">came off my snowboard</font> and hurt myself. That's never really gotten better.
**D**: And I understand you've had about 10 visits to the clinic over the last two years. So that's your right wrist and shoulder?
**P**: Mm-hmm. And other joints too.
**D**: Tell me about this pain in your shoulder. Is there anything that makes it feel worse?
**P**: Well, I like to do a lot of exercise, but that definitely makes it worse. Like if I do a set of <font color="#ff0000">pull-ups</font> in the gym or <font color="#ff0000">rowing</font> or if I'm using the <font color="#ff0000">free weights</font>, pretty well any sort of exercise I do, I find the pain's pretty bad.
**D**: Right.
**P**: And it's not just when I'm in the gym. It's <font color="#ff0000">constant all through the day</font>. There's no fluctuation. So I never get a break from it. It's really <font color="#ff0000">got me down</font> and no one seems to know what to do about it. They've diagnosed various different things and I have to lie on my left side at night or the pain <font color="#ff0000">disturbs my sleep</font>. So that's not easy.
**D**: Mm-hmm. So apart from that, are you generally in good health?
**P**: I suppose so. But what I've noticed is that my right wrist sometimes feels <font color="#ff0000">cold</font>.
**D**: OK.
**P**: And there's no reason I can see for that. And the same for my <font color="#ff0000">left knee</font>. It suddenly goes cold. It's very strange. When I told the doctor about that, he checked to see if the <font color="#ff0000">blood flow</font> was OK. And it was.
**D**: Right.
**P**: And he asked if I felt any <font color="#ff0000">numbness</font> there, but I don't.
**D**: OK. That's quite strange. And have you had any medical problems in the past?
**P**: Well, the only thing really is I used to have really bad <font color="#ff0000">acne</font>. It started when I was about 13. And I got it on my face. And then it spread to my back. And nothing I did seemed to have any effect on it. Like, first, my mom went to the <font color="#ff0000">pharmacy</font>. And she got this stuff I rubbed on. I can't remember the name. It was something <font color="#ff0000">peroxide</font>.
**D**: <font color="#ff0000">Benzoyl peroxide</font>?
**P**: Yeah, that's it. But that didn't do any good. So my mom took me to the doctor. And he gave me <font color="#ff0000">antibiotics</font>. And they didn't do much either. Then I read this article that said it can be caused by your diet. And you should <font color="#ff0000">cut out dairy products</font>. So I did that for a bit. But it just got worse. ... So then my doctor referred me to the hospital. And I saw a <font color="#ff0000">dermatologist</font>. ... And she prescribed me some stuff that was much stronger. And that did have an effect for a bit. But I got really dry <font color="#ff0000">lips</font>. And they started <font color="#ff0000">cracking</font> and bleeding.
**D**: OK.
**P**: And another side effect… Every so often, I started getting this awful <font color="#ff0000">depression</font>. So in the end, I stopped taking anything. And it seems to have sorted itself out.
**D**: Yes, your skin looks fine now. So let's have a look at this shoulder of yours. … That is the end of Part A.

### 2.2 Q13–24 答案与分析

| # | 答案 | 原文依据 | 考法 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 13 | rollerblading（轮滑） | I took a fall while I was rollerblading | 直接抓词 |
| 14 | shoulder（肩） | I hurt my right shoulder here | 直接抓词 |
| 15 | snowboard（滑雪单板） | when I came off my snowboard | "fell from a" = came off |
| 16 | pull-ups（引体向上） | if I do a set of pull-ups in the gym | 直接抓词 |
| 17 | sleep（睡眠） | the pain disturbs my sleep | 直接抓词（note "right side" 为干扰）|
| 18 | left knee（左膝） | the same for my left knee. It goes cold | note "right wrist and ___"，答案是另一部位 |
| 19 | numbness（麻木） | he asked if I felt any numbness | 拼写：-mb- 的 b 不发音 |
| 20 | peroxide（过氧化物） | It was something peroxide — Benzoyl peroxide? | note 印 Benzoyl，填 peroxide |
| 21 | dairy（乳制品） | you should cut out dairy products | dairy ≠ diary（日记）|
| 22 | dermatologist（皮肤科医生） | I saw a dermatologist… prescribed… stronger | "by ___" = 施动者填**人** |
| 23 | lips（嘴唇） | I got really dry lips. They started cracking | 直接抓词 |
| 24 | depression（抑郁） | I started getting this awful depression | 直接抓词 |

> [!tip] 临床背景（帮预判 + USMLE 视角）
> 痤疮阶梯用药链：**Benzoyl peroxide（过氧化苯甲酰，OTC over-the-counter 非处方）→ antibiotics（抗生素）→ dermatologist 开"更强的药"**——即 **isotretinoin（异维A酸）**。其副作用签名式组合正是原文的 **dry cracked lips（唇干裂/cheilitis 唇炎）+ depression（情绪障碍）**（USMLE 还高频考 teratogenicity 致畸 + iPLEDGE 双重避孕 + 血脂/转氨酶↑）。听力不必答病名，但认出这条链能帮你锁 Q20/23/24。皮肤科背景 → [[完整笔记/Peixuan分科笔记/derm]]。

## 三、做题技巧总结（从这两篇提炼）

> [!success] Part A 实战规则
> 1. **只认原音词，不许拿诊断常识反推**：Q6 neck 就是最惨教训——凭"银屑病=头皮"填 scalp 必错。疾病典型只能帮你**预判词性/缩小范围**，最终答案以听到的为准。
> 2. **答案严格按音频顺序出现**：手指跟着笔记走，听到锚点（how long / any other problems）就盯下一空。
> 3. **30 秒预读做三件事**：① 圈空格前后已给词（Q18 "right wrist and ___" → 听另一个部位）；② 圈语法线索（Q22 "by ___" → 填人不填药）；③ 把笔记心译成"要听什么"。
> 4. **同义转换是主考点**：fell from a → came off（Q15）、started with/initial → 原文先后（Q20）。笔记是转述不是听写。
> 5. **笔记内部线索能白送答案**：Q11 bladder 靠 "urine test"（尿检）就能推，不必等听清。
> 6. **拼写坑直接扣分**：dermatitis / non-allergenic / numbness（b 不发音）/ kneecaps / dairy（≠diary）。进 [[OET_听力_默写词库]] 默写。
> 7. **听懂临床套路 = 预判力**：Extract 1 = 皮肤问诊模板（主诉→分布→影响→既往干扰史→情绪→检查计划）；Extract 2 = 运动损伤 + 既往史模板（受伤史→加重因素→伴随异常→痤疮用药阶梯）。套路熟，长段落里答案位置能提前猜到。
