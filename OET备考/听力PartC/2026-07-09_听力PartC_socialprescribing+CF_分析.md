## 二、逐题拆解（Q31-42 全 12 题）

> [!info] 标记说明
> 每题标 ✅ 做对 / ❌ 做错；做对的也拆，看正确项如何**同义替换**原文、干扰项如何设坑。**做对 = success 绿色折叠**（复习扫过），**做错 = question 紫色折叠**（重点重做）。

> [!warning] 共性诊断
> **实际错 6 题**：Extract 1 独白段 4 题（31/32/34/35）+ Extract 2 访谈段 2 题（39/42）。另 **Q41 我做对了，标准 key 本身错**（见 §一）。
> 6 题同一根源：**正确项只认"音频明确说过的"，我却总多走一步**——被近处细节带偏、把"部分"夸大成"完全"、抓到一个真词就顺着编、或选了音频根本没讨论的维度。独白段（Ex1）尤其吃亏，因为没有主持人提问当路标。

### 【Extract 1 · 独白段】

### Q31 — 近处干扰陷阱

> [!question]- What does Dr Simms point out about the idea of social prescribing by GPs？
> **考点句（transcript 4-5）**："The project was inspired by the recent **initiative that encourages GPs to include social prescribing** in a patient's therapy."
>
> - ✅ **A. promoted as part of their practice** = "initiative encourages GPs to include it" 的同义改写
> - ❌ **B. developed in her own local area** —— 后文提到 local community garden（本地花园），但那是**她选的活动场地**，不是 social prescribing **理念的起源**。把"本地花园"误当成"理念本地首创"。
> - ❌ C. not well received —— 原文没说 GP 群体不接受。

### Q32 — 程度夸大陷阱（reduce ≠ no longer）

> [!question]- When Dr Simms first heard about social prescribing, she was worried that…
> **考点句（transcript 9）**："I was worried that the effect might be to **reduce the input of GPs** in patient care and **cut back on the amount of direct contact** GPs have with them."
>
> - ✅ **C. allocated less time with her patients** = "cut back on direct contact"（减少直接接触 = 更少时间）
> - ❌ **A. no longer have a say in treatment** —— 原文是 "reduce the input"（减少参与），A 说 "**no longer** have a say"（完全没有发言权）。**reduce ≠ no longer**，程度被夸大。
> - ❌ B. patients won't want to see her —— 原文没提。
>
> **反射**：听到 no longer / never / always 等绝对词，先警惕是否夸大了原文的"部分/减少"。

### Q33 ✅ 做对 — 一系列健康益处

> [!success]- Why did Dr Simms choose a gardening project for her patients？（答 B）
> **考点句（transcript 16-18）**："My hope was that the activity would improve their **physical health**... But I also hoped that being out in the fresh air... would have a **positive emotional effect** on them too."
>
> - ✅ **B. offered them a range of health benefits** = physical health + emotional effect（**身+心两方面** = a range of benefits）
> - ❌ A. previous patients had done it —— 原文没提以前的患者做过。
> - ❌ C. emphasis on physical fitness appealed to them —— **偷换 + 张冠李戴**：她之前推过 keep fit sessions（健身课）但**失败了**；园艺的好处是身心双重，不是"健身吸引了他们"。
>
> **为什么做对值得记**：正确项 B 把原文两句（physical + emotional）**归纳**成 "a range of health benefits"——Part C 正确项常是**概括归纳**，不是逐字复现。

### Q34 — 近处细节钓鱼（伤病 ≠ 怕受伤）

> [!question]- Initially, Dr Simms was concerned that her patients on the gardening project might…
> **考点句（transcript 20）**："I was concerned that the **hard work involved in digging** a garden might be a **daunting prospect** for some of them, particularly those who had physical limitations."
>
> - ✅ **A. put off by the demanding nature of the work** = "hard work... daunting prospect"（繁重的活令人望而却步）。daunting = 令人却步 = put off。
> - ❌ **B. injure themselves** —— 紧接着提到 knee surgery（膝手术）、back pain 不能 bend/stoop，这些**生理限制**很容易脑补成"怕受伤"。但她**明说**的担忧是"活太重、令人却步"；那些伤病细节是用来给项目经理**量身调整活动**（transcript 23），不是担心受伤。
> - ❌ C. scale of the project —— 原文没提"项目规模"。
>
> **反射**：扣"她**明说**担心什么"，别拿题干附近的细节脑补。

### Q35 — 主语偷换（医生想法 ≠ 患者反馈 / 一人 ≠ 三人）

> [!question]- Dr Simms was pleased, in their feedback, all three of her patients mentioned…
> **考点句（transcript 26-30）**："**All** reported… they'd not expected to **enjoy being out in the fresh air** as much as they eventually did. This was the most welcome news."
>
> - ✅ **A. how much they enjoyed spending time outdoors** = "all… enjoy being out in the fresh air"（**三人都**提到享受户外）
> - ❌ B. satisfaction of completing task —— transcript 30 "**one patient** added that he left… feeling it was a job well done"，是**一个人**说的；题干问 **all three mentioned**，不满足。
> - ❌ **C. willingness to take part in future projects** —— transcript 32 "It all **boded well for initiatives we might** go on to set up" 是**她（GP）自己的**展望，不是**患者反馈**。题干限定 "in **their feedback**"。
>
> **反射**：盯题干限定词 —— all three（不是一人）、their feedback（患者说的，不是医生想的）。

### Q36 ✅ 做对 — 取决于 GP 的核心作用

> [!success]- Looking back, Dr Simms understands that the success of social prescribing depends upon…（答 C）
> **考点句（transcript 37-40）**：患者说 "without your **gentle push**, I'd never have considered it"，并 "**trusted my expertise** and viewed my social prescription as another part of his **treatment**."
>
> - ✅ **C. the central role played by the GP** = "gentle push" + "trusted my expertise" + 把处方当治疗（成功靠**医生**推动与信任）
> - ❌ A. type of activity selected —— 她选了园艺，但顿悟点不是"选什么活动"。
> - ❌ B. attitude of individual patients —— 关键是**GP 的推动**，不是患者态度。
>
> **为什么做对值得记**：整段用一个患者原话来点题，正确项抓的是话里的**功能**（GP 的 push/信任），不是表面词。

### 【Extract 2 · 访谈段】

### Q37 ✅ 做对 — 早年即需治疗（干扰：hospital visits 是负面的）

> [!success]- Dr Hussein says that patients with cystic fibrosis…（答 B）
> **考点句（transcript 53）**："symptoms of the condition begin to **present very early on in the patient's life**, many of my patients are young people."
>
> - ✅ **B. tend to require treatment from a young age** = "symptoms present very early on / young people"
> - ❌ A. find the side effects alarming —— 没提副作用。
> - ❌ C. improve their condition with frequent hospital visits —— hospital visits 有提到，但患者是 "finding these hospital visits **frustrating**"（负面）；**提到 ≠ 正面**，别把出现过的词当正确项。

### Q38 ✅ 做对 — 不懂治疗的重要性（因无即时后果）

> [!success]- What does Dr Hussein say is difficult when treating patients who are not in hospital？（答 C）
> **考点句（transcript 61-62）**："if patients stop taking their medication... their condition **won't immediately worsen**. Instead, it will gradually become more severe until they contract a serious infection."
>
> - ✅ **C. don't always understand the importance of their treatment** = 因为停药"不会马上恶化"，患者以为能 **get away with it**（蒙混），没意识到重要性
> - ❌ A. find the exercises too complicated —— 没说"太难"。
> - ❌ B. difficulty socializing —— 那是后面 Q41 的话题；**别把别处的真信息搬来答这题**。

### Q39 — 真词 + 假 claim（抓到真词就顺着编）

> [!question]- What does Dr Hussein say about the role of distraction therapy in cystic fibrosis treatment？
> **考点句（transcript 66-67）**："We use the VR (virtual reality, 虚拟现实) headsets to transport the patient to outdoor settings, often **corresponding to the activities they're required to do** with us... breathing exercises on a stationary bike → VR 显示 a virtual outdoor bike ride."
>
> - ✅ **A. can be related to the treatment being provided** = "corresponding to the activities"（VR 场景**对应**真实治疗内容）
> - ❌ **B. complete activities more quickly**（我选的）—— 音频里有 "**complete** breathing exercises" 的 complete 这个真词，但**从没说"更快 quickly"**。抓到一个真词就给它安了原文没有的"快"。
> - ❌ C. gives staff more info about condition —— 音频说 VR 让患者放松、更易施治 administer，不是"提供病情信息"。
>
> **反射**：正确项只认音频**明确说过**的动作/关系，别顺着一个真词补出原文没有的意思。

### Q40 ✅ 做对 — 开始享受治疗（干扰：respiratory improvement）

> [!success]- What changes did Dr Hussein observe in one of her patients？（答 C）
> **考点句（transcript 74-76）**：那位 24 岁男患者起初抗拒，一旦 got on board，"breathed new life into him... he actually **began to look forward to it**."
>
> - ✅ **C. began to enjoy aspects of their treatment** = "began to look forward to it"
> - ❌ A. showed respiratory improvement —— 说的是**心态**变化（开始期待治疗），没提呼吸 / 生理好转；别把"好转"默认成"呼吸好转"。
> - ❌ B. deteriorated rapidly following a change —— 正好相反（是变好了）。

### Q41 ✓ 我做对了（标准 key 错，该给分）

> [!success]- Dr Hussein plans to use technology to help CF patients to communicate…（我答 A，正确 A）
> **考点句（transcript 81、83）**："they can't talk to **those going through the same thing**" + "a network of **fellow sufferers**"（病友、同病）。
>
> - ✅ **A. with other patients who suffer from the condition**（同病）= "fellow sufferers / same thing"
> - ❌ B. with family members —— 原文是**病友之间**交流，不是家属。
> - ❌ C. with patients suffering from a **different** condition —— 与 "same thing" 直接矛盾。
>
> ⚠️ **红色标准 key 判 C 是错的**：原文白纸黑字 "same thing / fellow sufferers"，只能是 A。这一分该给我。

### Q42 — 无中生有（凭"改善"脑补"没那么痛"）

> [!question]- Dr Hussein suggests that future treatments for cystic fibrosis…
> **考点句（transcript 90-91）**：gene therapy 用健康基因替换致病突变 → "effectively **cure** those with the condition... extend the lives... **remove the need for lengthy stays in hospital**."
>
> - ✅ **B. could prevent breathing difficulties [for] sufferers** = 基因治疗根治 → 从源头消除黏液 mucus / 呼吸问题
> - ❌ A. always incorporate lengthy procedures —— 正好相反，原文是 **remove** lengthy hospital stays。
> - ❌ **C. might be less painful than the current option**（我选的）—— 音频**通篇没提"疼痛 pain"**。把"未来更好"脑补成"没那么痛"，但正确项扣的是"**预防/根治**"，跟减痛无关。
>
> **反射**：音频没讨论的维度（这里是 pain）绝不能选——哪怕它"听起来像个进步"。

> [!tip] 一句话总纲

## 四、录音转录（faster-whisper medium，本人录音）

> [!note]- Extract 1 — Social prescribing（Georgia Sims, GP）
> My name's Georgia Sims and I'm a GP working at a practice in a small country town. My special interest is community mental health and my presentation today is on the subject of what's called social prescribing. I've been involved in a community health project which has been using this and which seems to be making a difference to the well-being of some of my patients.
>
> The project was inspired by the recent initiative that encourages GPs to include social prescribing in a patient's therapy. GPs are now able to write a prescription that allows a patient to attend one of a number of activities, each of which focuses on sociability and the feeling of belonging to a community. The activities encourage patients to link in with non-medical sources of support in both indoor and outdoor contexts.
>
> When I first heard about social prescribing, I had some concerns, but I was still keen to explore how it might be of help to my patients who suffer from moderate levels of depression and anxiety. I identified three long-term patients who often spoke of loneliness and feelings of isolation in addition to their mental health issues. But I was a little worried that the effect might be to reduce the input of GPs in patient care and cut back on the amount of direct contact GPs have with them. But after reading about various social prescribing projects that had been underway for a while, I realized this wasn't the case. Indeed, I began to see that my having the ability to prescribe a social activity would be helpful to my patients.
>
> Although there are several activities that lend themselves to social prescribing, like walking or singing in a community choir, I decided to go with a gardening project for several reasons. I'd already been contacted by the manager of a well-established community garden, which is tended by volunteers. Unfortunately, some volunteers had recently moved out of the area, and more needed to be recruited. The garden is located close to public transport, which was an important consideration, as none of my patients are able to drive for medical reasons.
>
> My hope was, of course, that the activity would improve their physical health. I'd previously encouraged them to try keep fit sessions, but this hadn't been a success. But I also hoped that being out in the fresh air in a non-threatening environment would have a positive emotional effect on them too.
>
> I was cautiously optimistic about the benefits to my patients of becoming involved in the gardening project. However, I was concerned that the hard work involved in digging a garden might be a daunting prospect for some of them, particularly those who had physical limitations related to health conditions. For example, one patient had undergone knee surgery the previous year and was understandably cautious about digging. Another patient has chronic back pain and was therefore unable to bend or stoop down. It was important to make sure the project manager was aware of their limitations and could tailor the activities to suit the patient's abilities, and reassure them that the activities were indeed within their capabilities.
>
> After the first month of the project, I spoke to each of the three participants individually and was pleased by their feedback. All reported that they were enjoying an enhanced sense of wellbeing. They all admitted that when I first talked to them about the gardening project, they'd not expected to enjoy being out in the fresh air as much as they eventually did. This was the most welcome news for me. All other attempts I'd made to get them involved in such activities had been unsuccessful. One patient added that he left the gardening project each week feeling that it was a job well done, and I was very happy to hear that all three felt they had more self-confidence and felt that the future was brighter for them as a result of taking part. It all boded well for initiatives we might go on to set up.
>
> Looking back, I realised that I'd been unnecessarily sceptical about prescribing activities that were already available in the community. After all, I'd reasoned, people have been able to join all sorts of community projects on their own for a long time. Why would a GP's prescription make a difference? Something that one patient said, however, brought home to me the value of social prescribing. When I asked him how he felt about being involved in the gardening project, he said, without your gentle push, I'd never have considered it. When I asked him to explain, he said that it was like any other prescription I gave him for his condition. He trusted my expertise and viewed my social prescription as another part of his treatment. This, above all, has convinced me that social prescribing is a valuable addition to a range of mental health therapies.

> [!note]- Extract 2 — Cystic fibrosis（Dr Hussein 访谈）
> **[主持人]** You hear an interview with a physician called Dr Hussein who's talking about treating patients with cystic fibrosis.
>
> Cystic fibrosis is a condition that causes mucus to be thicker and stickier than it should be. Dr Hussein specializes in cystic fibrosis treatment and is here to share her thoughts on caring for people with the condition. Can you tell us a bit more about patients who suffer from cystic fibrosis?
>
> **[Dr Hussein]** Absolutely. Sufferers tend to carry two to five times as much salt in their bodies as those without the condition. So you can see why their mucus is thicker than average. Treatment for these patients is usually quite time consuming and repetitive. Patients are often required to stay in hospital for long stretches. And as the symptoms of the condition begin to present very early on in the patient's life, many of my patients are young people. And so we tend to see lots of patients with cystic fibrosis finding these hospital visits frustrating.
>
> **[主持人]** In fact, throughout the UK, about 80 percent of patients with cystic fibrosis who are hospitalized report feeling at least minimal levels of depression. How about young patients who aren't currently hospitalized? What can be challenging about their treatment?
>
> **[Dr Hussein]** Well, patients can be required to take around 30 pills a day to keep cystic fibrosis under control. So it's understandable that teenagers and young people who just want to be free and independent might resent this ordeal if they think they can get away with it. One of the most difficult things we have to contend with is the fact that if patients stop taking their medication or doing their daily breathing treatments, their condition won't immediately worsen. Instead, it will gradually become more severe until they contract a serious infection, which puts their lives at risk.
>
> **[主持人]** So what approaches do you use when treating patients with cystic fibrosis?
>
> **[Dr Hussein]** Well, we've found distraction therapy to be extremely useful. We're incredibly lucky to have received a donation of a number of virtual reality headsets following their success in a number of treatment trials. We use the virtual reality headsets to transport the patient to outdoor settings, often corresponding to the activities they're required to do with us. When they complete breathing exercises on a stationary bike, for example, the VR headsets display a virtual outdoor bike ride. Our patients find it helpful to pretend to be somewhere else during treatment. And it's often easier for us to administer breathing treatments to patients using these headsets, as they're more relaxed when they're not focused on the actuality of the test.
>
> **[主持人]** So what sorts of changes have you seen in your patients as a result of these methods?
>
> **[Dr Hussein]** One of my patients, a 24-year-old man with cystic fibrosis who was in hospital waiting for a lung transplant, found treatment very difficult at first. He was preoccupied by his need for a transplant and frustrated by feelings of powerlessness. He would often resist treatment. We started using the virtual reality systems with him as soon as we got them, and it took a while for him to get on board. But when he did, it was like someone had breathed new life into him. Not only did he stop hindering his treatment, he actually began to look forward to it. He's even started helping us to think about other ways we can improve the experiences of our patients, like improving social interaction.
>
> **[主持人]** Yes, I understand that there are difficulties involved in patient communication.
>
> **[Dr Hussein]** We're looking into the possibility of instant messaging functions between patients and even virtual games that they can play against each other. Unfortunately, patients with cystic fibrosis have to be kept apart to avoid cross-infection. It's just one more cross to bear for our patients that they can't talk to those going through the same thing.
>
> **[主持人]** Right.
>
> **[Dr Hussein]** Our patients get plenty of interaction with myself and the rest of the staff, but we'd like to make sure they have access to a network of fellow sufferers, too, for support and advice.
>
> **[主持人]** That all sounds quite futuristic. Are there any other advances on the horizon for the treatment of cystic fibrosis?
>
> **[Dr Hussein]** Well, there's a new drug that's been in the news recently. It's a combination of lumacaftor and ivacaftor. You might know it by the brand name Orkambi. The drug works by improving the level of water and salt in the body, thereby reducing the thick mucus that causes illness and respiratory issues in those with cystic fibrosis. Even more exciting and futuristic, though, is the possibility of gene therapy, where the genetic mutation that causes cystic fibrosis in individuals is replaced with a healthy gene. This would effectively cure those with the condition and significantly extend the lives of thousands of people and remove the need for lengthy stays in hospital.

> [!tip] 转录校订说明
> - 药名原音频 = **lumacaftor（鲁玛卡托）+ ivacaftor（依伐卡托）**，品牌 **Orkambi（奥卡姆比）**——Whisper 误转成 "lumicafta and ivacafta...or Kambi"，已订正。这是 CFTR modulator（CFTR 调节剂）组合，机制描述（改善水盐→稀释黏液）与真实药物相符。
> - 医生名 "Hussein" 为低把握转写，人名不影响做题。
