## Extract 1：Dr Marsh 谈运动性脑震荡 concussion（Q31-36）

### Q31 新指南的 aim（正 C，我选 B ❌）

原文证据：
> "the aim was really to provide a resource, **not for the top level professional sports people, but for parents, teachers and coaches** of young people playing sport. The guidelines basically offer some **expert information from a GP, an emergency physician and myself as a neurosurgeon**..."

- ✅ C "provide a range of **specialist** advice"：GP (general practitioner, 全科医生) + 急诊医生 emergency physician + 神经外科医生 neurosurgeon = **a range of specialists**（同义替换：expert information from 三种专科 → range of specialist advice）
- ❌ A "educate **young sportspeople**"：受众错位——资源是给 **parents/teachers/coaches** 的，不是给年轻运动员本人（原文明确 not for... but for...）
- ❌ B "correct misunderstandings"：原文 "difficult for teachers, parents to work out if someone is okay... we hope to **remedy that**"——remedy 的是"家长不会判断"这个**实际困难**，不是纠正"误解 misconception"。没有任何"大家理解错了"的表述

> [!warning] 陷阱类型：受众/对象错位
> Part C 爱把"给谁用"和"内容是什么"拆成两个选项。听到 not for X but for Y 结构必须记下 Y。

### Q32 得过脑震荡的人会……（正 C，我选 A ❌）

原文证据：
> "**not all patients actually lose consciousness**... signs may clear initially, **but then return when the individual undertakes further physical activity**... people shouldn't start playing again **until those warning signs have completely subsided**"

- ✅ C "take precautions to avoid the risk of **symptoms recurring**"：症状会在恢复运动时**卷土重来**（return = recur），所以要等完全消退才能回去 = 采取预防措施
- ❌ A "be unconscious for varying amounts of time"：**被原文直接否定**——"not all patients actually lose consciousness"（不是所有人都会失去意识）
- ❌ B "need a medical examination"：原文只说等症状消退，没提必须体检

### Q33 过早回归危险的原因（正 A，我选 B ❌）⭐ 本段最经典陷阱

原文证据：
> "**It used to be thought** that receiving another concussion could lead to severe brain swelling and that **could be fatal**... I think **the evidence is fairly slim for that**. What we **do** know, though, is that the **compounding effect** of having one concussion followed by another seems to be more severe than just the one."

- ✅ A "a subsequent episode can have a **cumulative** effect"：compounding effect = cumulative effect（复利式叠加 = 累积效应）
- ❌ B "high risk of **fatality**"：**"过去认为"陷阱**——fatal 确实出现了，但包在 "It used to be thought..."（过去曾认为）里，紧接着被 "the evidence is fairly slim"（证据很薄弱）否定。这是 Part C 最高频的出题手法：**旧观点原词做干扰项，新观点换装做正确项**
- ❌ C "brains need time to return to normal size"：brain swelling 被扭曲成 "return to normal size"，而且 swelling 本身就是被否定的旧理论

> [!danger] 听力反射：It used to be thought / previously / traditionally = 后面马上有翻转
> 听到这类信号词，**旧观点里的抓眼词（fatal/swelling）100% 会出现在干扰项里**。真答案在 "What we do know, though..." 之后。

### Q34 脑震荡风险主要在于……（正 A，我选 C ❌）

原文证据：
> "people playing **sports like rugby, where there's bodily contact, stand more chance** of being at the receiving end of such a blow. **But having said that, it's just as likely to affect** kids kicking a ball around a park **as** it is to affect top professional players in big matches."

- ✅ A "lies mainly in the **choice of sports** played"：风险主要取决于**玩什么运动**（rugby 等身体接触型运动机会更大）
- ❌ C "greater when played in **less formal situations**"：having said that 那句说的是"公园踢球的孩子和职业球员**一样可能**（just as likely）"——是**拉平**，不是"非正式场合**更高**"。just as... as = 等号，不是大于号
- ❌ B "reduced by good playing technique"：全文没提技术

> [!warning] 我当场问过这句话还是选错了
> "having said that" 是让步转折：先说 rugby 更危险（→A 成立），再补充"但业余职业一样会中招"（→场合无关，C 不成立）。**对比级别（more chance）指向运动种类，等级比较（just as likely）排除场合**。

### Q35 青少年赛事要不要医疗支持（正 B，我 B→C 改错 ❌）

原文证据：
> "most games, even the most dangerous ones, are without incident at all. I think people involved in **running youth sports, whether they be referees, coaches or parents, can be made aware of how to manage concussion**... But I think **always having a doctor on the sidelines... is just an overreaction**."

- ✅ B "The **organisers** should be capable of dealing with any issues"：referees/coaches/parents = 组织赛事的人（running youth sports → organisers），can be made aware of how to manage = capable of dealing with
- ❌ C "medical professionals should be encouraged to **volunteer**"：全文没有任何"志愿"字眼；医生常驻场边被明确定性为 **overreaction**（反应过度）
- ❌ A "risky enough to justify it"：被 "even the most dangerous ones are without incident" 否定

> [!danger] 改答案教训（第一直觉 B 是对的）
> 你的第一直觉抓住了 "referees, coaches or parents can be made aware"。改成 C 大概率是被 "doctor" 这个词回响干扰——但原文对 doctor 的态度是**否定的**（overreaction）。UWorld 你已有改答案错 188 题的数据，OET 同样适用：**没有新证据不改**。

### Q36 美国大学橄榄球的新进展（正 A，我选 A ✓ 但不确定）

原文证据：
> "They're **trialing helmet sensors and impact sensors**... I don't think it'll come to that. I think there are **two scenarios**... The second... involves **chronic traumatic encephalopathy**. This comes about **particularly in American football** where players use their helmets and heads almost like weapons. That type of repeated impact seems to **add up over the player's career**... But **I see that as a different matter to the management of concussion**."

- ✅ A "only really addresses an issue which is **particular to that sport**"：CTE (chronic traumatic encephalopathy, 慢性创伤性脑病) 特发于美式橄榄球（头盔当武器、反复撞击累积）——传感器针对的是这个**该运动特有**的问题，和普通脑震荡管理是 a different matter
- ❌ B "only benefit professional players"：原文只说职业层面关注度更高，不是传感器只惠及职业
- ❌ C "significant step forward in **all sports**"：被 "I don't think it'll come to that"（不会推广到所有地方）直接否定

## Extract 2：Dr Skelton 讲 SPRINT 血压试验（Q37-42）

> [!info] 名称速记
> SPRINT (Systolic Blood Pressure Intervention Trial, 收缩压干预试验)；对照提及的 ACCORD (Action to Control Cardiovascular Risk in Diabetes, 糖尿病心血管风险控制试验)。BP = blood pressure (血压)。

### Q37 试验为何提前终止（正 A，我选 A ✓）

原文证据：
> "The intention was to follow these patients for **five years**... the trial was stopped after just **three years**, thanks to an **all-cause mortality reduction of nearly 30%** for the 120 group, which was **definitive** and shocking, but wonderful."

- ✅ A "conclusive results earlier than expected"：definitive = conclusive；3 年 < 计划 5 年 = earlier than expected
- ❌ B dropout：原文 "factoring in the usual dropout rate" 是**设计时已考虑**的常规脱落率，不是停止原因
- ❌ C 安全担忧：结果是 "wonderful"，不是 concern

> [!tip] USMLE 交叉加成
> 这就是 RCT (randomized controlled trial, 随机对照试验) 的 **early stopping for benefit**（因获益提前终止）：继续让对照组维持高目标已不伦理。Biostats/Ethics 都可能考。

### Q38 部分 75 岁以上参与者退出的原因（正 C，我 B→C 改对 ✓）

原文证据：
> "Others thought there would be a **failure to take the number of tablets** needed... **Again, this wasn't an issue.** The average needed was just three per day... Participants from this age group who didn't finish the trial were taken out because **some conditions, which were already present, worsened**. For example, obesity levels rose too high."

- ✅ C "health deteriorated due to **pre-existing conditions**"：conditions already present = pre-existing（已存在的疾病）; worsened = deteriorated
- ❌ B "failed to take required doses"：**又是"提到即否定"**——服药依从性担忧被 "this wasn't an issue" 明确排除。你第一选 B 正是掉进这个坑，**改成 C 改对了**，这次的改动有原文证据支撑（"Again, this wasn't an issue" + "taken out because..."），是正确的改法
- ❌ A "negative impact on daily life"：dizzy spells 影响 wellbeing 的担忧 "turned out to be **unfounded**"（被证明没根据）

### Q39 测血压方式的特别之处（正 B，我选 B ✓）

原文证据：
> "an **automated machine** was used **at the hospital**. This took three separate readings and **averaged** them. Also, readings were taken **while staff were out of the room** to avoid what's called **white coat syndrome**."

- ✅ B "the patient was **alone** when it was carried out"：staff out of the room = 患者独自在场（防白大衣高血压）
- ❌ A "the **highest** of three readings"：数字扭曲——是**平均值 averaged**，不是最高值
- ❌ C "done **manually** by participant **at home**"：双重反转——是 automated（不是 manually）、at the hospital（不是 at home）；at-home ambulatory monitoring 是被 "Rather than..." 排除的方案

### Q40 SPRINT vs ACCORD 的区别（正 B，我选 A ❌）⭐ 方向反转题

原文证据：
> "**Accord was about half the size of Sprint**, and **unlike Sprint, the Accord study allowed diabetic patients** to take part. Despite this, in general, **the Accord participants were rather lower risk than those in the Sprint trial**."

三个选项全在考**主语方向反转**（问的是 SPRINT，原文句子的主语全是 ACCORD）：

| 原文（主语=ACCORD） | 翻转成 SPRINT 视角 | 选项判定 |
|---|---|---|
| Accord 是 Sprint 的一半大 | **SPRINT 更大** | A "SPRINT had fewer participants" ❌ 反了 |
| Accord 允许糖尿病人参加，unlike Sprint | **SPRINT 不含糖尿病人** | C "SPRINT included diabetics" ❌ 反了 |
| Accord 参与者 lower risk | **SPRINT 患者风险更高** | B "SPRINT involved higher-risk patients" ✅ |

> [!danger] 听力反射：比较句必做"主语翻转"
> 题干问 A，音频说 B（"B was half the size of A" / "unlike A, B allowed..."）——**每一句都要在脑内把主语换回题干问的那个**。选 A 是把 "half the size" 直接安到了 SPRINT 头上。

### Q41 Dr Skelton 的主要保留意见（正 B，我选 C ❌）

原文证据：
> "importantly, one which was **sponsored by government rather than by the interests of pharmaceutical corporations**. **I recommend a note of caution, though, as Sprint does contradict previous findings.** The Cochrane view in 2011... said that lowering to under 140 didn't produce a change in the risk of death overall."

- ✅ B "results **go against the existing body of evidence**"：contradict previous findings（Cochrane 2011）= go against existing evidence；信号词 "**a note of caution, though**" 直接标出 reservation 所在
- ❌ C "unduly influenced by **pharmaceutical companies**"：**极性反转陷阱**——pharmaceutical corporations 确实出现了，但方向是**正面的**：政府资助、**不是**药企资助（rather than）。这是给试验加分的点，不是保留意见
- ❌ A "ignores wider implications"：没说

> [!danger] 听力反射：听到题干关键词 ≠ 找到答案，先判极性
> Q33（fatal 被否定）、Q41（pharma 是优点不是缺点）同一性质：**词对上了，方向反了**。抓 though / but / rather than / turned out to be unfounded 这些极性词。

### Q42 SPRINT 对未来的影响（正 C，我选 B ❌）

原文证据：
> "a few **GPs have already been working with older patients to hit lower blood pressure goals**, and the new data will doubtless **encourage greater take-up of this more interventionist line of attack**... it's **unlikely that lifestyle changes alone would be enough** – it could well require several anti-hypertensive drugs... it **doesn't mean that a new universal standard** for blood pressure is in order."

- ✅ C "GPs will adopt a **more active approach** to lowering BP in the **elderly**"：more interventionist line of attack = more active approach；older patients = the elderly；greater take-up = 更多 GP 采用
- ❌ B "increased attention to **lifestyle**"：**又是极性反转**——lifestyle 被提到，但结论是"光靠生活方式**不够**，还得加好几种降压药"。你选 B 是第三次掉同一类坑
- ❌ A "universally applicable guidelines"：被 "doesn't mean a new universal standard is in order" 直接否定

```
│   Q41  pharma 是"政府资助而非药企"= 优点
│   Q42  lifestyle 被 "unlikely to be enough" 否定
│   Q32  unconscious 被 "not all patients lose consciousness" 否定
│
├─ ② 比较句主语反转 —— 1 题
│   Q40  原文全说 ACCORD，选项全问 SPRINT，需逐句翻转
│
└─ ③ 对象/程度错位 —— 2 题

```

> [!tip] 下次 Part C 三条听力反射
> 1. **听到题干里的原词先警惕，不要伸手**——真答案几乎总是同义替换（compounding→cumulative / definitive→conclusive / staff out of the room→alone / interventionist→active）；**原词复现常是干扰项**
> 2. **抓极性信号词**：It used to be thought / though / but / rather than / this wasn't an issue / turned out to be unfounded / I don't think it'll come to that——后面才是考点
> 3. **比较句做主语翻转**：题干问 X，音频以 Y 为主语描述时，每句翻转回 X 再对选项

## 难句解析（本篇 4 个复杂句式）

**1. 让步转折 + 同级比较**
> But **having said that**, it's **just as likely to** affect kids kicking a ball around a park **as** it is to affect top professional players in big matches.

having said that = 话虽如此（对前句 rugby 更危险做让步修正）；just as... as = 两边**完全等同**；kicking a ball around a park 现在分词后置定语（around 表随意玩玩）。

**2. "旧观点→翻转"结构（Part C 万能考点模板）**
> **It used to be thought that** X could lead to severe brain swelling and that could be fatal... I think **the evidence is fairly slim for that**. **What we do know, though, is that** the compounding effect... seems to be more severe.

三段节奏：旧观点（used to be thought）→ 否定（evidence is slim）→ 新结论（What we do know, though, is...，do 是强调助动词）。答案永远在第三段。

**3. Rather than 排除结构**
> **Rather than** give patients an arm cuff for at-home 24-hour ambulatory monitoring, an automated machine was used at the hospital.

Rather than + 动词原形 = "不是……而是……"，前半句是**被排除的方案**（却常被做成干扰项 C "manually at home"）。

**4. 双重否定式限定**
> it **doesn't mean that** a new universal standard for blood pressure **is in order**. **Instead**, it's a good reason for everyone to discuss... their own particular target.

be in order = 是恰当的/该出台的；doesn't mean... Instead... = 否定普适、转向个体化——正对 Q42 干扰项 A。

### Extract 1 — Dr Ian Marsh 谈运动性脑震荡访谈（Q31-36）

**Interviewer:** My guest today is Dr. Ian Marsh, a specialist in the treatment of concussion in sport and a co-author on a new set of guidelines. So, Dr. Marsh, what's the aim of these new guidelines?

**Dr Marsh:** Well, the aim was really to provide a resource, not for the top level professional sports people, but for parents, teachers and coaches of young people playing sport. The guidelines basically offer some expert information from a GP, an emergency physician and myself as a neurosurgeon about what the condition is, also how to identify the symptoms and how to manage it. If any of your listeners have ever had a concussion doing sports, you'll know how frightening it can be. It's confusing and painful and difficult sometimes for teachers, parents or whoever to work out if someone with concussion is okay. I mean, we hope to remedy that.

**Interviewer:** And how do we know when someone is suffering from concussion?

**Dr Marsh:** Well, obviously, if the person's actually knocked out, it's clear, but not all patients actually lose consciousness. Often following a hard knock to the head, they become disorientated or experience headaches, nausea or vomiting. And these are signs of concussion and they may clear initially, but then return when the individual actually undertakes further physical activity, when they start to train, say. So it can actually take quite a while for things to really clear up.

**Interviewer:** The essence of it is that people shouldn't start playing again until those warning signs have completely subsided.

**Dr Marsh:** Yes. And you say that waiting anything less than 14 days after all the symptoms have cleared would be too early to return. *[说话人归属含糊，听感像主持人接话或 Marsh 复述指南原文——不影响答题]*

**Dr Marsh:** Yeah, that's right. If they go back too early, they risk a second concussion. And as we know from professional athletes, they may have to give up their sport if they have too many concussions. So it's better, particularly in a young person with a developing brain, to allow all of the symptoms to settle and only then return to play. Well, usually return to train first, then return to play after that.

It used to be thought that receiving another concussion could lead to severe brain swelling and that could be fatal or at least involve a visit to the emergency room. I think the evidence is fairly slim for that. What we do know, though, is that the compounding effect of having one concussion followed by another seems to be more severe than just the one. So it's always better to let the brain recover fully before playing again.

**Interviewer:** Right. So who's at the highest risk of sports concussion?

**Dr Marsh:** Well, actually, a concussion can happen whenever anyone receives a blow to the head. Usually it's a sort of twisting blow, not a straight-on blow. But obviously, people playing sports like rugby, where there's bodily contact, stand more chance of being at the receiving end of such a blow. But having said that, it's just as likely to affect kids kicking a ball around a park as it is to affect top professional players in big matches.

**Interviewer:** Do you think that youth sports need specialist concussion doctors on hand, like the professionals do?

**Dr Marsh:** There's always a risk and we know that it happens from time to time. But I mean, most games, even the most dangerous ones, are without incident at all. I think people [who] are involved in running youth sports, whether they be referees, coaches or parents, can be made aware of how to manage concussion — the signs that they need to look out for and maybe the warnings of something more serious — so that they can take the appropriate actions. But I think always having a doctor on the sidelines where young people are playing is just an overreaction.

**Interviewer:** Right. In the USA, college football is big business. They're trialing helmet sensors and impact sensors. Do you think that's something we need everywhere?

**Dr Marsh:** Well, I don't think it'll come to that. I think there are two scenarios here. The first is one where a concussion is a one-off event following a significant blow to the head. The second is quite different and involves chronic traumatic encephalopathy. This comes about particularly in American football where players use their helmets and heads almost like weapons. That type of repeated impact seems to add up over the player's career. That's something we've heard being discussed, mostly in the USA. Naturally, there's interest generally in protecting players, particularly in the professional levels of sport. But I see that as a different matter to the management of concussion.

### Extract 2 — Dr Pamela Skelton 讲 SPRINT 血压试验讲座（Q37-42）

*[Extract Two 开场引导语被广告盖住，原转录为 "but extract to I'm Dr."]*

**Dr Skelton:** I'm Dr. Pamela Skelton, consultant cardiologist at this hospital. And I'm talking about the recent SPRINT study into the effects of setting lower blood pressure targets, which in turn affects the advice and medication which patients are given. I'm going to describe the patients who were selected, how the trial was conducted and the implications of its results for us all as health professionals.

First, the trial itself. It involved over 9,000 hypertensive participants aged 50 plus, most of whom were on blood pressure medication. They were randomly assigned to one of two groups. One with a goal of less than 120 millimetres systolic BP, the other with a goal of less than 140 millimetres, the traditional standard. The intention was to follow these patients for five years, factoring in the usual dropout rate. As it turned out, however, the trial was stopped after just three years, thanks to an all-cause mortality reduction of nearly 30% for the 120 group, which was definitive and shocking, but wonderful.

As I mentioned, the participants were over-50s. And it goes without saying that as people age, they develop more diseases and health problems as a matter of course. But there was a specific group of over-75s who did just as well as younger patients. Before the trial, some medics referred to the natural stiffening of the arteries with ageing, suggesting that 120 was too low a target for the over-75s, risking an increase of dizzy spells, which would affect general wellbeing. But this concern turned out to be unfounded. Others thought there would be a failure to take the number of tablets needed to reach a BP of 120, especially among older participants. Again, this wasn't an issue. The average needed was just three per day. The over-75s already on various drugs didn't object to extra medication. Participants from this age group who didn't finish the trial were taken out because some conditions, which were already present, worsened. For example, in some cases, obesity levels rose too high.

To manage their blood pressure, participants were given standard drugs, nothing experimental, just drugs that are readily available and low cost. Another key factor was that blood pressure was measured in a very specific way. Rather than give patients an arm cuff for at-home 24-hour ambulatory monitoring, an automated machine was used at the hospital. This took three separate readings and averaged them. Also, readings were taken while staff were out of the room to avoid what's called white coat syndrome in patients.

Now, some of you may be familiar with the ACCORD study into blood pressure levels several years ago, which in some respects was similar to SPRINT. There are some differences, though. For example, ACCORD was about half the size of SPRINT, and unlike SPRINT, the ACCORD study allowed diabetic patients to take part. Despite this, in general, the ACCORD participants were rather lower risk than those in the SPRINT trial, probably because of the slightly lower average age. The ACCORD trial didn't show a statistically significant benefit for overall cardiovascular outcomes, but there was a clear 40% reduction in strokes, even though that was a secondary outcome.

So, to summarise, the SPRINT trial seems to support 120 as a recommended blood pressure target. This is doubtless a landmark study, and importantly, one which was sponsored by government rather than by the interests of pharmaceutical corporations. I recommend a note of caution, though, as SPRINT does contradict previous findings. The Cochrane view, in 2011, for example, said that lowering to under 140 didn't produce a change in the risk of death overall. However, we must bear in mind that Cochrane was looking retrospectively at trials which weren't actually focused on the same particular issue. So it's worth doing a full and systematic evaluation to see where the SPRINT trial fits in with what we already know.

It's interesting that a few GPs have already been working with older patients to hit lower blood pressure goals, and the new data will doubtless encourage greater take-up of this more interventionist line of attack. But the SPRINT results don't mean that everyone with hypertension should be dropping to under 120. Plus, to achieve those lower levels, it's unlikely that lifestyle changes alone would be enough — it could well require several anti-hypertensive drugs as well. There remain some unanswered questions, of course, for example, whether other groups, like those with a lower heart attack risk, need to keep their blood pressure that low. So while SPRINT can help guide doctors' decisions about some patients, it doesn't mean that a new universal standard for blood pressure is in order. Instead, it's a good reason for everyone to discuss with their doctor their own ideal and particular target.
