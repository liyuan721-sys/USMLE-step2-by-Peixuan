## 二、逐题拆解（Q37-42 全 6 题）

> [!info] 标记说明
> 每题标 ✅ 做对 / ❌ 做错；做对的也拆，看正确项如何**同义替换**原文、干扰项如何设坑。**做对 = success 绿色折叠**（复习扫过），**做错 = question 紫色折叠**（重点重做）。

> [!warning] 共性诊断
> 错 4 题（38/39/41/42），根源**高度一致**、也与既往 Part C 错题同源：**选项在原文里"真实出现过"就选，没有先扣死题干问的是哪一层**。38 问 involves → 选了没出现的"专业发展"方向；39 问 surprising → 选了普通 benefit；41 问 doing → 选了效果 equitable care；42 问 advises → 选了 result 护士满意。反射：**先锁题干动词（involves / surprising / doing / advises），再回原文找"回答这一层"的句子。**

### Q37 ✅ 做对 — 距离影响生存率（干扰：support ≠ 消除路程）

> [!success]- Joanna says that the main aim of the IPF project was to…（答 A）
> **考点句（transcript）**："regional centres… can be a considerable **distance from patients' homes**. We realised that **survival rates were being affected** because a lot of patients **couldn't manage the long trip**, and that prompted the project to **set up more sites**."
>
> - ✅ **A. make patient journey times shorter** = 本地多设点（set up more sites）→ 缩短就医路程
> - ❌ B. provide better quality transport —— 从没提改善交通工具。
> - ❌ C. support patients **on their way** to and from centres —— 陷阱：项目是**消除**长途奔波（本地设点），不是在路上"陪同/支持"他们，方向反了。
>
> **反射**：aim = 解决"the long trip"这个痛点 = 缩短路程，不是美化路程。

### Q38 ❌ 做错（选 A）— involves 什么：向有经验者学，不是"发展主题"

> [!question]- Joanna says that the project involves…（正确 C，我错选 A）
> **考点句（transcript）**："We then **invited everyone who had expressed an interest** to our hospital to see how our **nurse-led clinic monitors patients**… Our nurses' **knowledge and experience was shared**, so more staff are now in a position to set up new clinics."
>
> - ✅ **C. providing opportunities to learn from experienced staff** = knowledge/experience was shared + 参观 nurse-led clinic（观摩护士主导门诊）
> - ❌ **A. persuading nurses to develop professionally**（我选的）—— 两处错：① 护士是自己 "**expressed an interest**"（主动感兴趣）来的，**不是被 persuade**；② 原文讲的是"**分享经验让人会开新诊所**"，不是"推动护士做**专业发展**"。我被"nurses / more staff"这些词带出一个原文没有的"职业发展"方向。
> - ❌ B. encouraging colleagues to adjust their career path —— 没提改变职业路径。
>
> **反射**：project **involves**（项目包含什么）→ 找原文描述的**具体动作**（分享经验、观摩门诊 = learn from experienced staff），别顺着"nurse"这个词脑补成"发展护士"。

### Q39 ❌ 做错（选 C）— surprising 要扣 "unanticipated"，不是普通好处

> [!question]- Which aspect of the project does Joanna find most surprising?（正确 A，我错选 C）
> **考点句（transcript）**："I think the most positive outcome, and one that was **totally unanticipated**, has been the **dramatic rise in adherence** to the anti-fibrotic [medication]（抗纤维化药物依从性大幅上升）."
>
> - ✅ **A. improvements in compliance with treatment** = rise in **adherence**（依从性 = compliance）；被 "totally **unanticipated**"（完全没预料到）点名 = 最意外。
> - ❌ **C. more consistent access to expert care**（我选的）—— 电话门诊确实让患者更快用上服务（consistent access），但这被她归为 "**but we were kind of expecting that**"（在意料之中）→ 不是 surprising。我选了一个**真实好处**，但漏了题干问的是"**最意外**"的那个。
> - ❌ B. the rapid uptake of the new services —— 同样属于"意料之中"的好处，非意外。
>
> **反射**：问 "most **surprising**" → 只认信号词 "totally unanticipated / didn't expect"；凡她说 "we were expecting that" 的一律排除。别把"好处"默认成"意外的好处"。

### Q40 ✅ 做对 — 老年患者听不清（struggle to catch = hearing impaired）

> [!success]- What does Joanna suggest as a downside of the project?（答 B）
> **考点句（transcript）**："given their typical age and pre-existing conditions, some patients do **struggle to catch everything that the nurses are saying**."
>
> - ✅ **B. It may be unsuitable for the hearing impaired** = struggle to catch what nurses are saying（听不清）—— 典型 OET 抽象化改写。
> - ❌ A. unable to provide round-the-clock assistance —— email support 是 "**five days per week**"，但她框成**优点**（在电话之外的补充），不是缺点。
> - ❌ C. no face-to-face contact —— 她主动反驳："even though the support isn't given in person as such, it's **still very much tailored**"（虽非面对面但仍个性化）→ 她说这**不算**问题。
>
> **反射**：downside 要找她**明说**的缺点（听不清），别被 5-day / no in-person 这些她已"洗白"的点带走。

### Q41 ❌ 做错（选 A）— equitable ≠ inclusive，问 doing 找动作本身

> [!question]- What is Joanna most enthusiastic about doing in future?（正确 B，我错选 A）
> **考点句（transcript）**："We've also created a **structured care pathway** for staff to use with patients **throughout the disease trajectory**… This is **really exciting**, as it aims to provide health professionals with **evidence-based [guidelines] to follow**… ensuring all patients receive **equitable**, high-quality care at each phase of the disease."
>
> - ✅ **B. improving IPF management procedures** = structured care pathway + evidence-based guidelines for staff to follow throughout the disease = **一套贯穿病程、给医护遵循的管理流程**
> - ❌ **A. introducing a more inclusive treatment system**（我选的）—— 我把 "equitable"（公平/一视同仁）当成 "inclusive"（纳入更多人群，如照顾不同群体/边缘患者）的同义词。**两个概念不同**；而且 equitable care 是这套 pathway 的**效果**，题目问的是她热衷**做什么**（= care pathway 本身）。
> - ❌ C. developing existing service centres —— 她要 "**expand the model across the country**"（拓展到新地区），不是发展现有中心。
>
> **为什么错值得记（双重教训）**：
> 1. **词义硬划等号**：equitable（公平）≠ inclusive（包容/纳入更多人）。抓到一个"听起来对"的抽象词就配同义选项 = 老毛病（同 07-09 篇 Q39/Q42 "顺词编义"）。
> 2. **doing 找动作本身，不找效果**："really exciting" 指向的**动作** = 建 structured care pathway（管理流程 = B）；"equitable, high-quality care" 是这套流程的**卖点/目的**，不是答案。题干问 "most enthusiastic about **doing**" → 锁她**做的那个东西**。
>
> **反射**：题干问"做什么"→ 找她着手做的**具体东西/流程**；漂亮的结果词（equitable / high-quality / better outcomes）是**诱饵**，别拿它去配选项。

### Q42 ❌ 做错（选 B）— advice 是"立协议文件"，不是"护士满意"（result）

> [!question]- Joanna advises anyone thinking about setting up a similar project to ensure that…（正确 A，我错选 B）
> **考点句（transcript）**："you need to take it one step at a time, **drawing up a protocol document** with shared information about patient demographics, pulmonary function tests, schedule monitoring, etc. at the beginning is **vital**…"
>
> - ✅ **A. staff have clear procedures to follow** = protocol document（协议文件 = 清晰的操作流程）；她明说 "at the beginning is **vital**" = 她给的**建议**。
> - ❌ **B. nurses' job satisfaction is a high priority**（我选的）—— "Nurses report being more **fulfilled**"（护士更有成就感）确实在原文里，但那是项目的**结果 / 附带好处**，**不是她给别人的建议**。我又一次把"结果"当成了"建议"（和 Q41 把"效果"当"doing"同一个病）。
> - ❌ C. cases are processed as quickly as possible —— 原文有 "make informed decisions… **quickly**"，但那是 protocol 带来的**下游好处**，建议本体是"立协议文件"。
>
> **反射**：advises… to **ensure that** = 找她**建议去做**的那个动作（立 protocol document）；护士满意 / 处理快都是它的**结果**，不是建议本身。

> [!tip] 一句话总纲

> [!warning] 干扰项通用套路（本套复现）
> - **把"效果/结果"当"动作/建议"**：Q41 equitable care（效果）、Q42 processed quickly（好处）、B 项 nurses fulfilled（结果）—— 题干问 doing / advises to ensure，要锁**她做的那个东西**。
> - **她已"洗白"的点**：Q40 的 5-day support、no in-person —— 被她框成优点/反驳掉的，不是 downside。
> - **在意料之中 ≠ surprising**：Q39 rapid uptake "we were expecting that" → 排除。

## 四、录音转录（faster-whisper medium，本人录音）

> [!note]- Extract 2 — IPF project（Joanna Parker 访谈）
> **[主持人 Mark]** My guest today is Joanna Parker, a senior respiratory nurse, who'd set up a new project to help patients with idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis, or IPF. Joanna, could you tell us a bit about the project and what your aim was in setting it up?
>
> **[Joanna]** Sure. IPF is a debilitating condition that affects about 33,000 people in the UK. Sadly, there's no cure, and it's a life-limiting progressive disease characterised by a decline in lung function, so it has a dramatic and negative impact on patients' lives. They need access to palliative care and help with managing their symptoms. These services are usually delivered at regional centres, which can be a considerable distance from patients' homes. We realised that survival rates were being affected because a lot of patients couldn't manage the long trip, and that prompted the project to set up more sites. The average age of an IPF patient is 74, and many have significant comorbidities and also require oxygen therapy. So travelling can be very challenging, particularly if they're using the bus or train. Not only that, but the treatments can cause liver toxicity, so patients need monitoring every one to three months.
>
> **[主持人]** So how do you go about it, and what exactly did it involve?
>
> **[Joanna]** First, we discussed our concerns with our regional IPF network and identified essential referring areas. Next, we contacted specialist nurses in those areas to find out whether they were interested in collaborating with us to set up local monitoring clinics, and the response was hugely positive. We then invited everyone who had expressed an interest to our hospital to see how our nurse-led clinic monitors patients' treatment. For example, we did one particularly valuable workshop on managing the side effects of medication, which include gastrointestinal disturbance, photosensitivity and fatigue. Our nurses' knowledge and experience was shared, so more staff are now in a position to set up new clinics.
>
> **[主持人]** And have there been any pleasant or interesting surprises?
>
> **[Joanna]** Well, a key feature of the project has been the introduction of telephone clinics, and we've found that these have been absolutely pivotal for operation across the region. Without them, patients wouldn't be able to get the specialist support they need. They've also meant that patients are referred far more quickly and can utilize services such as palliative care when they really need them, but we were kind of expecting that. I think the most positive outcome, and one that was totally unanticipated, has been the dramatic rise in adherence to the anti-fibrotic [medication]. It seems that the extra support has made all the difference. Also, in a snapshot survey we did, there was a unanimous response that the patients themselves prefer the new local monitoring sites and see them as very valuable.
>
> **[主持人]** And have there been any problems associated with the new project?
>
> **[Joanna]** Well, it's not perfect. The nurse-led telephone clinics do mean that consultations can take place from the comfort of the patient's own home, and that's wonderfully convenient. That said, given their typical age and pre-existing conditions, some patients do struggle to catch everything that the nurses are saying. But otherwise, I'd say it's extremely effective. It doesn't allow anybody to fall through the net. We introduced an IPF nursing team's secure email account so that patients could access help and support from us five days per week. This is in addition to telephone contact. So even though the support isn't given in person as such, it's still very much tailored to individual patients.
>
> **[主持人]** Do you have any plans to take the project further, or are you going to leave it at that?
>
> **[Joanna]** Looking ahead, we'd like to expand the model across the country. That would mean developing the network of health care professionals involved, finding out about other available resources in the area, and expanding our clinic provision further. We've also created a structured care pathway for staff to use with patients throughout the disease trajectory, and we're hoping to start rolling that out in the near future. This is really exciting, as it aims to provide health professionals with evidence-based [guidelines] to follow, ensuring all patients receive equitable, high-quality care at each phase of the disease. It should have a huge impact on patient outcomes.
>
> **[主持人]** And finally, what advice would you give to anyone considering setting up a similar project in their region?
>
> **[Joanna]** Well, you need to take it one step at a time, drawing up a protocol document with shared information about patient demographics, pulmonary function tests, schedule monitoring, etc. at the beginning is vital, as it offers extra support and enables people involved in the project to make informed decisions independently and quickly. Nurses report being more fulfilled, too, mainly because it's made referral decisions easier and everyone's knowledge of local patient services has improved. And finally, remember that the secret to success is communication. Communication is the key.
