## 二、逐题拆解（Q37-42 全 6 题）

> [!info] 标记说明
> 错题（37/38/41）用 `[!question]-` 紫色折叠（重点重做），做对题（39/40/42）用 `[!success]-` 绿色折叠。每题都拆：正确项如何**同义替换**原文、干扰项如何设坑。

### Q37 ❌ 做错（选 C）— surprises = staff 的态度（陷阱：policies variation 没提）

> [!question]- What surprises Jennifer about the issue of hand-hygiene routines?（正确 B，我错选 C）
> **考点句（transcript）**："why do we continue to see such an **underutilization** of such a basic practice?... this figure doesn't tend to **set off any real alarm bells** in people... it's a number that quite often reflects **human neglect and forgetfulness**. One may even suggest at times **laziness**... even now after centuries of knowledge... we're still in many ways **ridiculing it by simply overlooking** this basic practice."
>
> - ✅ **B. the attitude of many healthcare staff towards them** = neglect / forgetfulness / laziness / overlooking / 不 set off alarm bells = 医护人员**对手卫生的态度**让她惊讶。
> - ❌ **C. the variation in policies designed to promote them**（你选的）—— 她说 policies 是 "**strict policies and guidelines that we must all abide by**"（严格、都得遵守），**没提政策有"差异/不一致"**。你把"有政策"错读成"政策有差异"。**这就是你错的点**。
> - ❌ A. the range of conditions that can result from neglecting them —— 开头列的 Staph aureus / C. difficile / MRSA / gram-negative 是**背景铺垫**，不是让她"surprise"的点。
>
> **反射**：surprises → 找她带**情绪**反复讲的东西（这里是对 staff 忽视态度的不解/失望）；strict policies 是她陈述的事实，不是"政策差异"。

### Q38 ❌ 做错（选 C）— 数据 support 的是 complacency（陷阱：把绝对数字读成"增加趋势"）

> [!question]- Jennifer mentions recent data on infection levels to support her point that…（正确 A，我错选 C）
> **考点句（transcript）**："one in 25 patients acquires a healthcare associated infection... about **700,000 infections a year**... 75,000 patients die from their infections... it's a number that quite often reflects **human neglect and forgetfulness**. One may even suggest at times **laziness**."
>
> - ✅ **A. there appears to be complacency within the health sector** = neglect / forgetfulness / laziness = **自满/松懈**；她用触目惊心的数据反衬"大家却不当回事"（doesn't set off alarm bells）。
> - ❌ **C. there is an increase in the number of patients being affected**（你选的）—— 数据是**绝对数字**（1/25、700k、75k 死），**不是"增加趋势"**。原文没有 "rising / more than before / increase" 的比较级信号。你把"数字很大"误读成"数字在增加"。**这就是你错的点**。
> - ❌ B. there seem to be falling standards amongst healthcare staff —— "falling"（下降）同样是**趋势词**，原文没说标准在下滑，是说**一直**被忽视（centuries of knowledge 却仍 overlooking）。
>
> **反射**：数据题先看**有没有比较级/趋势词**（increase / falling / rising）。没有趋势词 → 排除 C、B；数据是为了反衬"态度"（complacency）= A。

### Q39 ✅ 做对 — 手卫生防感染是老概念（干扰：technology 不能取代 ≠ 被削弱）

> [!success]- Jennifer suggests that the concept that hand-hygiene can prevent infections…（答 B）
> **考点句（transcript）**："Stopping the spread of infection by way of hand hygiene is **hardly a new concept**. Its importance was first brought to life in the **mid 19th century**... **even now after centuries of knowledge**..."
>
> - ✅ **B. has long been accepted as essential to good healthcare practice** = hardly new / 19 世纪至今 / centuries of knowledge = **早已确立的基本共识**。
> - ❌ A. has been weakened by the introduction of new technology —— 她说的是 "advanced healthcare... can be made **redundant** if hand hygiene neglected"（技术**不能取代**手卫生），不是"技术**削弱**了这个概念"。方向不同。
> - ❌ C. has been more readily acknowledged in some branches of medicine —— 没提"某些科室更认可"这种**分科差异**。
>
> **反射**：hardly new + centuries = 老概念（B）；technology 那段是"再先进也白搭"，不是"概念被技术削弱"。

### Q40 ✅ 做对 — 简单便宜（干扰：so many types ≠ most commonly found）

> [!success]- Jennifer feels that hand hygiene is particularly valuable because…（答 C）
> **考点句（transcript）**："it works for **so many different types of organisms**, and you get a lot of **bang for the buck**... such great protection... by such a **simple and inexpensive action** that any person can do with ease."
>
> - ✅ **C. it is an uncomplicated procedure that comes at very little cost** = simple and inexpensive / bang for the buck = **简单、便宜**。
> - ❌ **B. it provides protection from the most commonly found organisms**（强干扰）—— 原文是 "so many **different types** of organisms"（种类**多**），**不是"最常见的"**（most commonly found）。"多种" ≠ "最常见"，偷换。
> - ❌ A. it may be easily imbedded in an individual's habitual behaviour —— habitual/automatic 是 **Q42** 的点，不是这里 valuable 的理由。
>
> **反射**：valuable because → 锁 "simple and inexpensive"（C）；"so many different types" 是数量多，别配成"most common"（B）。

### Q41 ❌ 做错（选 A）— 直接观察的问题 = 观察者影响行为（霍桑效应）

> [!question]- What problem does Jennifer identify with direct observation as a method of monitoring hand-hygiene compliance?（正确 B，我错选 A）
> **考点句（transcript）**："direct observation... the reliability... is **questionable**. This is because it **increases dramatically when observers... are circulating in the unit. The rates then decline almost immediately once they've gone**... being a **known** staff member also influences this... staff member observed **close to 70%**... the student observed **close to 30%**... a big **discrepancy**."
>
> - ✅ **B. The presence of observers can influence staff behaviour** = 有观察者在场 compliance 飙升、走了就掉 + 认识的人观察到 70% vs 陌生学生 30% = **员工因被（认识的）人观察而改变行为** = 霍桑效应（Hawthorne effect，被观察者因知道被观察而改变行为）。
> - ❌ **A. Observers tend to make subjective judgements**（你选的）—— 70% vs 30% 的 discrepancy **不是因为观察者"主观判断不同"**，而是**员工在不同观察者面前行为不同**（认识的人在场就认真洗）。主体是被观察的**员工**，不是观察者的判断。**这就是你错的点**。
> - ❌ C. Observers may not always provide a good model of compliance —— "good model"（观察者自己是否做好榜样）原文根本没讲。
>
> **反射**：这题问 direct observation 的 problem = **Hawthorne effect**（被观察者行为受观察影响）。看到"有人看就做得好、没人看就掉"直接锁 B（presence influences behaviour）；别被"discrepancy"带去"观察者主观"（A）。

### Q42 ✅ 做对 — 手卫生要变成自动动作（干扰：seat belt 不是 danger response）

> [!success]- Jennifer mentions car seat-belts to underline her point that hand hygiene…（答 A）
> **考点句（transcript）**："humans are not... naturally **hardwired** to perform hand hygiene... If compared with the use of **seat belts**... most people **don't even think about** putting the seat belt on... They do it on a **subconscious level**... That's where we need to be with hand hygiene, **hardwired so that we do it without even thinking about it**."
>
> - ✅ **A. needs to become an automatic action** = subconscious / hardwired / without thinking = **自动化、无意识的动作**。
> - ❌ B. is an instinctive response to personal danger —— seat belt 是**习惯性自动**（subconscious），不是"面对危险的本能反应"；类比点是 automatic，不是 danger。
> - ❌ C. needs to be a conscious decision on each occasion —— **正好相反**：她要的是**无意识/自动**，不是"每次都有意识地决定"。
>
> **反射**：seat belt 类比 = subconscious/automatic → hand hygiene needs to become automatic（A）；C 是反向陷阱（conscious ≠ subconscious）。

> [!tip] 一句话总纲（本篇最该记的）
> 3 道错题一个病根：**选了原文挂得上边的"具体表象"，漏了她整段真正强调的"态度 / 心理机制"那一层**。37 政策差异 vs staff 态度、38 患者增加 vs 自满 complacency、41 观察者主观 vs 员工被观察改变行为（霍桑效应）。**做题铁律：Part C 问 surprises / to support / problem 时，先问"她整段在反复渲染什么情绪或机制"（这里全篇在骂 neglect / complacency / 不当回事），答案通常就是那个"态度/机制"选项，不是顺带提到的名词。**

> [!warning] 干扰项通用套路（本套复现）
> - **具体表象 vs 态度/机制**：37/38/41 全踩——政策差异、患者增加、观察者主观都是"具体挂边点"，答案是态度（B）/自满（A）/行为机制（B）。
> - **趋势词陷阱**：38 的 C(increase)、B(falling) 都是**比较级**，原文只有绝对数字 → 排除。
> - **数量多 ≠ 最常见**：40 "so many different types" 被偷换成 "most commonly found"（B）。
> - **反向陷阱**：42 subconscious/automatic 的反面是 C(conscious decision each time)。
> - **跨题挪用**：40 的 A(habitual behaviour) 其实是 42 的点（automatic）——选项在别题的答案里，别错位。

## 五、录音转录（faster-whisper medium，本人录音）

> [!note]- Hand hygiene — Jennifer 讲座（Q37-42，monologue）
> ...[开头] We've all been taught since we were toddlers, hand hygiene. This is described by many healthcare workers as possibly the single most important tool in preventing the development of healthcare-associated infections between patients. Infections such as Staphylococcus aureus, Clostridium difficile, MRSA and gram-negative bacteria. Most hospitals have strict policies and guidelines that we must all abide by. So if it's so important, why do we continue to see such an underutilization of such a basic practice? Why don't we follow the procedures that should already be ingrained? This unfortunately has proven difficult to determine.
>
> Let's first put things into perspective with new data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention in the USA, which found that one in 25 patients acquires a healthcare-associated infection during their hospital care. Now, for whatever reason, this figure doesn't tend to set off any real alarm bells in people, but that adds up to about 700,000 infections a year — a little more alarming. The clincher, however, is of those, 75,000 patients die from their infections. That's an incredible number and one that should shock anyone hearing it, because it's a number that quite often reflects human neglect and forgetfulness. One may even suggest at times laziness.
>
> Stopping the spread of infection by way of hand hygiene is hardly a new concept. Its importance was first brought to life in the mid 19th century when it was found that physicians who washed their hands before delivering babies prevented deaths in postpartum women. Such an idea was initially ridiculed, as most new theories are, but even now after centuries of knowledge, it seems to me we're still in many ways ridiculing it by simply overlooking this basic practice.
>
> In stopping the spread of infection, even the most advanced healthcare is of little use if the basic principles of hand hygiene are neglected. We have valuable technologies and intricate tools at our disposal to prevent hospital-acquired infections, and yet such advancements can so easily be made redundant if healthcare workers don't toe the line and simply neglect to wash their hands. That doesn't sound like genuine advancement to me.
>
> One of the most valuable things about hand hygiene is that it works for so many different types of organisms, and you get a lot of bang for the buck, so to speak. Such great protection from so many nasty things by such a simple and inexpensive action that any person can do with ease. The issue is that you have to practice it at a high level of compliance for it to work. Within a hospital setting, there are so many opportunities for hand hygiene, and it's difficult to get a level of compliance where we're able to make changes to infection rates. What we need to do to combat this is to turn hand washing into what many people already suppose it is — an instinctive ritual.
>
> So how do we monitor people in a healthcare setting without hovering and interfering? The gold standard in monitoring hand-hygiene compliance is direct observation. But the reliability of this method to measure true, actual compliance is questionable. This is because it increases dramatically when observers, who are usually well known to the staff, are circulating in the unit. The rates then decline almost immediately once they've gone. Furthermore, being a known staff member also influences this. I'll give you an example. In one study, we hired a student for hand-hygiene observation in the same unit as a well-known staff member. We found that the staff member observed close to 70% compliance, and the student observed close to 30%. The bottom line is that there's a big discrepancy there.
>
> The main problem associated with a lack of hand-hygiene compliance, I believe, is that humans are not, in fact, naturally hardwired to perform hand hygiene in the absence of sensing a substance on their hands. If compared with the use of seat belts, for example, most people don't even think about putting the seat belt on when they get into a car. They do it on a subconscious level, and they're usually not even aware they put it on. That's where we need to be with hand hygiene — hardwired so that we do it without even thinking about it. When a behaviour is hardwired like that, you can always sense when you didn't do it because you start to feel uncomfortable. It's a long process, however, to get to that end point...
