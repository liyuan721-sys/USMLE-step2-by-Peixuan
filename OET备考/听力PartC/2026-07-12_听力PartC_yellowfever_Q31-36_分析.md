## 二、逐题拆解（Q31-36 全 6 题，全对）

> [!info] 标记说明
> 错题（Q32 / Q33）用 `[!question]-` 紫色折叠（重点重做），做对题用 `[!success]-` 绿色折叠（复习扫过）。每题都拆：正确项如何**同义替换**原文、干扰项如何设坑。

### Q31 ✅ 做对 — 最初的兴趣点 = 传播方式（干扰：family = 持续投入非最初）

> [!success]- What first led Dr Tiznado to develop an interest in yellow fever?（答 C）
> **考点句（transcript）**："up to a week after symptoms appear, an infected person can **retransmit the infection to the mosquitoes** who bite them, forming a **seemingly endless cycle**. That was **the point that grabbed me and pulled me in**."
>
> - ✅ **C. the way in which it spreads** = 人→蚊再传播的循环（retransmit / endless cycle）；被 "the point that grabbed me" 直接点名 = **最初**吸引她的点。
> - ❌ B. a family connection with it —— 陷阱：儿子得病（son contracted）确实提到，但那是 "**and then what kept me involved**"（之后让她**持续投入**），不是 "**first led**"（最初引发）。题干动词 first led → 时间线上的**第一个**触发点。
> - ❌ A. its preventable nature —— "could have been avoided / a simple vaccine" 出现在讲儿子那段，是遗憾语气，非"最初兴趣来源"。
>
> **反射**：first led（最初）vs kept involved（持续）—— OET 爱用时间副词区分两个都真实存在的动机。锁 "grabbed me / pulled me in" 这个**第一触发**信号句。

### Q32 ❌ 做错（选 C）— 网上信息互相冲突（陷阱：rare 否掉 well-founded）

> [!question]- Dr Tiznado feels people fail to get vaccinated because of…（正确 B，我错选 C）
> **考点句（transcript）**："information... is readily available through the internet and social media, which sadly is where a lot of people gather their **so-called facts**... **With all good information, there's also misinformation**... Instead of communicating with medical professionals, the factual data is sometimes overlooked."
>
> - ✅ **B. the existence of conflicting accounts on websites** = "good information + misinformation" 并存于网络 = **互相矛盾的说法**（conflicting accounts）；"so-called facts" 也指向不可靠的网络信息。
> - ❌ **C. well-founded concerns about the extent of adverse reactions**（你选的）—— 两处被否：① 她说 side effects "**are rare**"，且顾虑来自 **misinformation**，不是 "**well-founded**"（有充分根据）；② 她根本不认为不良反应是真问题。**这就是你错的点**。
> - ❌ A. a lack of fact-based guidance from specialists —— 反了：她说 factual data / medical professionals 是**有的、要去问的**（vital），不是"缺乏专家指导"。
>
> **反射**：well-founded / fact-based 这类"正面限定词"要回原文验证——她明说 side effects rare + misinformation 驱动，直接判 C 的 "well-founded" 为假。

### Q33 ❌ 做错（选 A）— 年轻无经验解释他的决定（陷阱：虚拟语气 if progressed）

> [!question]- What does Dr Tiznado tell us about her son?（正确 B，我错选 A）
> **考点句（transcript）**："He was in the group that **overlooked the available data and my advice**, but he was only a **young adult at the time, so I can hardly blame him** for this. He was lucky."
>
> - ✅ **B. His age and inexperience may explain the decisions he made** = young adult + "hardly blame him"（因年轻而不忍苛责）= 年龄/经验不足解释了他忽视建议的**决定**。
> - ❌ **A. He was close to entering the second stage of the illness**（你选的）—— **虚拟语气陷阱**：原文 "**if** he progressed to that level, I **may** no longer have a son" = 假设条件（他并没进第二阶段）；紧接 "He was **lucky**"。听到 if / may have / would have 就要警惕别当事实。**这就是你错的点**。
> - ❌ C. He was quick to take advantage of effective treatment options —— 黄热病**无特效治疗**，他靠 lucky，不是"及时用有效治疗"。
>
> **反射**：条件句（if... may/would）≠ 已发生的事实。这是本篇你差点栽、也是 Part C 反复出现的坑——**虚拟语气不等于事件发生**。

### Q34 ✅ 做对 — 病例证实她"疫苗第一"的信念（干扰：education / spending 被她否掉）

> [!success]- Dr Tiznado believes recent cases of yellow fever in Brazil amongst tourists…（答 A）
> **考点句（transcript）**："**none of the 10 reported cases had received a vaccination**, and I firmly believe **this outweighs all other causes**... going unvaccinated has **little to do with mass education, government spending**, or the like."
>
> - ✅ **A. confirm her beliefs about the effectiveness of vaccination** = 10 例全未接种 + "outweighs all other causes" = **印证她"没打疫苗压倒一切原因"的核心信念**。
> - ❌ B. illustrate the need to educate travellers in how to obtain vaccination —— 直接反驳：她说 unvaccinated "has **little to do with mass education**"。
> - ❌ C. underline the value of further investment in the promotion of vaccination —— 同样被否："little to do with... **government spending**"（虽仍是 supporter，但不认为这是关键）。
>
> **反射**：她把 education / government spending 主动"降权"（little to do with），凡选项踩这两个词的一律排除；答案锚在 "outweighs all other causes" = 疫苗本身。

### Q35 ✅ 做对 — 其他措施人人都该做（干扰：even those vaccinated 秒杀 B）

> [!success]- Dr Tiznado says that other steps aimed at preventing yellow fever are…（答 C）
> **考点句（transcript）**："There are a number of prevention methods that **all people should take, even those who've had the vaccination**"（避蚊、DEET、长袖、蚊帐等）。
>
> - ✅ **C. a wise precaution for anyone travelling to affected areas** = all people / even vaccinated → **人人（含去疫区者）都该采取的明智预防**。
> - ❌ **B. unnecessary for people who have been fully vaccinated** —— 被 "**even those who've had the vaccination**" 一句直接秒杀（关键全称锚点）。这是本题最强干扰，正好被那半句反驳。
> - ❌ A. essential for those unsure of their vaccination status —— 缩小了范围；她说的是 **all people**，不是"只对不确定接种状态者必要"。
>
> **反射**：even / all / anyone 这类**全称词**往往是答案锚点——"even those who've had the vaccination" 同时是 C 的证据 + B 的反证。

### Q36 ✅ 做对 — 建议要通俗易懂（干扰：specialist → authorities 是钩子）

> [!success]- Dr Tiznado says that to be effective, advice about yellow fever vaccination needs to…（答 A）
> **考点句（transcript）**："people find the **topic and language of vaccination confusing**... make **specialist knowledge freely available** worldwide in a dedicated website with factual, evidence-based information **in a language everyone can understand**."
>
> - ✅ **A. be expressed in a very accessible way** = "in a language **everyone can understand**"（人人都懂的语言）= accessible（通俗易懂）；由 "find... confusing" 引出需求。
> - ❌ **B. clearly come from highly respected authorities** —— **词面钩子**："**specialist** knowledge" 出现，诱你配 authorities；但落点是"**通俗易懂的语言**"，不是强调"来源权威"。抓一个词就配同义选项 = 要警惕。
> - ❌ C. be posted on a wide range of social media platforms —— 反了：她要 "a **dedicated website**"（专门网站），且全篇对社媒是**负面**态度（misinformation 就来自那里）。
>
> **反射**：字面出现 specialist ≠ 答案是 authorities。答案走同义改写（everyone can understand = accessible）；原词复现的选项多半是诱饵。

## 三、错题 + 陷阱三条速记

| 题 | 你选的 | 为什么是坑 | 需要的反射 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 33 ❌ | A 差点进第二阶段 | 虚拟语气 "if he progressed... I may no longer have a son"＝**假设**，他没进 | if / may have / would → **不是事实**，别当已发生 |
| 32 ❌ | C 对不良反应的充分担忧 | side effects "**are rare**" + 由 misinformation 驱动 → 不是 well-founded | well-founded / fact-based 要回原文验证真假 |
| 36 ✅（差点）| B 来自受尊敬的权威 | 字面出现 "specialist" 是钩子，落点是"人人能懂的语言" | specialist ≠ authorities；**原词复现＝诱饵** |

> [!tip] 一句话总纲
> 4/6：错的 Q32、Q33 都被**原文真实出现、但后文否掉的干扰项**骗了（side effects concerns / 差点进第二阶段）；对的 Q35 抓住 "even those vaccinated" 秒掉 B、Q36 没把 specialist 当 authorities。**做题铁律：先扣题干动词（first led / because of / tell us about / believes / are / needs to），再回原文找"回答这一层"且**同义改写**的句子——原词出现 ≠ 答案；限定词（well-founded）和条件句（if...may）回原文验证真假。**

> [!warning] 干扰项通用套路（本套复现）
> - **时间副词区分双动机**：Q31 first led（最初 = 传播方式）vs kept involved（后续 = 儿子）。
> - **虚拟语气 ≠ 事实**：Q33 "if he progressed... may no longer have a son"，他其实 lucky、没进第二阶段。
> - **她主动降权的因素**：Q34 mass education / government spending "little to do with" → 踩这些词的选项排除。
> - **全称词做答案锚**：Q35 "even those who've had the vaccination" 同时证 C、反 B。
> - **原词复现＝诱饵**：Q36 specialist（诱 authorities）；答案在 "language everyone can understand"（accessible）。

## 五、录音转录（faster-whisper medium，本人录音）

> [!note]- Yellow fever — Dr Tiznado 访谈（Q31-36）
> **[开头截断]** ...humans, and also monkeys, by infected mosquitoes in parts of Central and South America, and also Africa. It takes about three to six days for the illness to begin after the initial mosquito bite. But what's so interesting to me is that up to a week after symptoms appear, an infected person can actually retransmit the infection to the mosquitoes who bite them, forming a seemingly endless cycle. That was the point that grabbed me and pulled me in, so to speak. And then what kept me involved was the fact that my son, who was 22 at the time, then contracted this disease just after my research had begun. The distressing part for me, not only as a doctor, but a mother, was that this could have been avoided if only he'd taken the recommended precautions. A simple vaccine.
>
> **[主持人]** If the vaccine for yellow fever is available, why are people avoiding it?
>
> **[Dr Tiznado]** It's highly recommended to all travellers who plan to visit particular regions and the large majority follow this advice. However, individual beliefs and personal choice greatly impact this, which include concerns over serious side effects, which I should point out are rare. In this day and age, information on vaccination is now readily available to all through the internet and social media, which sadly is where a lot of people are gathering their so-called facts, which then lead to their decision-making. With all good information, there's also misinformation, and this is the dangerous part because that misinformation is often included in the formation of people's beliefs. Instead of communicating with medical professionals, which is vital, the factual data is sometimes overlooked, and that's when people run the risk of potential infection.
>
> **[主持人]** Now I know your son fortunately survived. Can you tell us more about his case?
>
> **[Dr Tiznado]** He was in the group that overlooked the available data and my advice, but he was only a young adult at the time, so I can hardly blame him for this. He was lucky. Yellow fever is characterised by two stages, and in the first, symptoms such as headache, fever, and muscle pain are common. About 15 to 25% then progress to the second stage, known as the toxic stage, and if he progressed to that level, I may no longer have a son. Of those who do enter the second stage, half die within 10 to 14 days after onset of the illness.
>
> **[主持人]** Brazil has often been labelled a high risk for contracting yellow fever. Can you give any statistics?
>
> **[Dr Tiznado]** In 2017, there were 10 travel-related cases of yellow fever from tourists who'd visited Brazil. I should say that none of the 10 reported cases had received a vaccination, and I firmly believe this outweighs all other causes for contracting the disease. Of the 10, half were from Argentina and Chile. The other five were from parts of Europe. This suggests to me that going unvaccinated has little to do with mass education, government spending, or the like, although I'm still a big supporter of such methods. I believe it comes back to a combination of individual choice, fear campaigns, and misinformation, but the bottom line is again, vaccination, and to a lesser extent, a belief in the "it won't ever happen to me" scenario.
>
> **[主持人]** For those who don't have the vaccination, are there other steps to take to prevent contracting yellow fever?
>
> **[Dr Tiznado]** Of course, and doing so saves countless lives. There are a number of prevention methods that all people should take, even those who've had the vaccination. Avoiding mosquitoes is the most obvious of these. Those that transmit yellow fever are actually most active during the day. Wearing repellent containing DEET and light-coloured long-sleeved clothes is a must. Leave the perfumes and colognes at home as they can attract mosquitoes, as well as using screens to prevent them from entering your accommodation. Even at night, a mosquito net is recommended. These are all good safeguards, and I'm certainly not entirely dismissing them, but to me, they are still a gamble. Vaccination is hands down the superior method, which, by the way, lasts for a lifetime.
>
> **[主持人]** So what needs to be done to get the correct information across?
>
> **[Dr Tiznado]** Education always has and always will be our most valuable tool, but people find the topic and language of vaccination confusing. Of course, more needs to be done to get the message across to all people, and a positive way to achieve this is to make specialist knowledge freely available worldwide in a dedicated website with factual, evidence-based information in a language everyone can understand.
